L, 114-116 – A Jamal Crawford jumper with less than a second remaining gave the Suns a 116-114 win over the Bucks on Nov. 23 at Fiserv Forum. After a back and forth first half, the Bucks trailed by 12, 91-79, with 4:09 remaining in the third quarter. But from there, Milwaukee closed out the quarter on an 11-0 run to pull within one. After Phoenix regained an eight-point lead at 108-100, in the fourth quarter, Milwaukee responded again, using a 14-0 run to take a six-point lead at 114-108 with 3:26 remaining. However the Suns were able to tie the game at 114 on a 3-point play by T.J. Warren before Crawford hit the game winner with eight-tenths of a second left. Khris Middleton’s game-winning 3-point attempt on the other end came just after the buzzer, giving the Suns the victory. Giannis Antetokounmpo led the Bucks scoring 35 points with 10 rebounds while Devin Book led the Suns, scoring 29 points. W, 106-97 – The Bucks took down the Pacers on Feb. 13, 106-97, picking up their seventh consecutive road win. Milwaukee led for the majority of the first quarter and the entirety of the second quarter, going up by as many as 13 points before taking an eight-point lead into halftime, 55-47. The Pacers opened the third quarter with a 12-3 run to go up by one, 59-58, and ultimately took a three-point advantage into the fourth quarter at 79-76. Indiana extended its lead to as many as 10 points after a quick 7-0 run, forcing the Bucks to take a timeout with 9:35 left to play. Following the break, Milwaukee outscored Indiana by 11 points to take the lead, 91-90, with 5:56 to play. The game would see four more lead changes before a Khris Middleton three-pointer and a Pacers turnover sealed the win for the Bucks. Giannis Antetokounmpo led the way with 33 points, 19 rebounds and 11 assists, notching his fifth triple-double of the season. Bojan Bogdanovic led the Pacers with 20 points. *  Milwaukee took down Indiana in the third game of the season series, 106-97, on 2/13, to take a 2-1 lead in the 2018-19 season series.
